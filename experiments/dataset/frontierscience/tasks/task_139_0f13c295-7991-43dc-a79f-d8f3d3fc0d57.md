## Prompt

Consider a plasmonic system where Ag nanocubes of edge length ( a = 75 ) nm are dispersed on a silicon substrate. The extinction spectrum of these nanocubes shows broad plasmon peaks, particularly at 532 nm and 785 nm. The quantum efficiency of charge excitation from the nanoparticle to MB depends on the plasmon resonance wavelength and photon intensity.
Question: Using the following assumptions and data, calculate the rate of photochemical decomposition of MB at 785 nm and compare it to the rate at 532 nm illumination, showing all necessary steps, and assuming both lasers have an intensity of ( 2.0 \\times 10^{20} ) photons/cm²/s.

1. The electromagnetic enhancement factor (( \\Sigma )) for the plasmonic field is ( 4 \\times 10^4 ) for both wavelengths.
2. The charge excitation cross-section at 785 nm is ( 3 \\times 10^{-18} ) cm², and at 532 nm it's ( 1 \\times 10^{-18} ) cm².
3. The initial rate of MB photochemical decomposition under direct laser illumination (no plasmonic enhancement) is ( 0.1 ) molecules/s at 785 nm illumination and ( 0.02 ) molecules/s at 532 nm illumination.
4. Assume photon-induced heating is negligible, and all photochemical activity is due to charge excitation.
