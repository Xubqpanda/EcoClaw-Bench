## Prompt

Context: The α7 nicotinic receptors are homopentameric proteins composed of five identical subunits arranged around the central pore.

You have several molecular simulations of the α7 nAChR in the resting and open states. These are not long enough to capture the full dynamics of full receptor transitions, but may be enough to capture the dynamics of one subunit. You are given a set of features which consist of the intra-subunit Cα distances and the inter-subunit Cα distances with the adjacent subunit (A with B, B with C, C with D, D with E, and E with A).
Question: Propose a computational strategy, explicitly identifying a primary method, to systematically reduce the dimensionality of this dataset while capturing the slowest dynamical modes governing the transition between states. Your approach should explicitly address the following:
\\begin{itemize}
\\item Symmetry Integration: How can the five-fold structural symmetry of the receptor be incorporated into the analysis to improve the efficiency and accuracy of the model?

\\item Dimensionality Reduction: How can the symmetry-adapted eigenvectors be used to project the high-dimensional feature space into a low-dimensional representation that preserves collective motions?
\\end{itemize}

Include a discussion of how symmetry constraints influence the estimation of dominant dynamical modes and how differences between symmetric subunits can be quantified or visualized post-reduction.
