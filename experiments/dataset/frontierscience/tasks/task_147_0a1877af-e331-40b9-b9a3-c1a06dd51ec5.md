## Prompt

Context: Information contained in DNA is transcribed to RNA and finally into proteins. The information in RNA needs to be encoded accurately for homeostasis and health. 

However, DNA and RNA are constantly exposed to damaging agents. DNA and RNA undergo oxidative damage from reactive oxygen species (ROS) reacting with nucleobases.
Question: **Part 1)**
A study compared healthy individuals with Alzheimer's Disease (AD) patients. Less than 2% of the total neuronal mRNA pool in healthy individuals was immuno-precipitated by an 8oxoG antibody. In AD patients, more than 50% of the total neuronal mRNA pool was immuno-precipitated. Is it possible to explain this discrepancy?

**Part 2)**

If ROS levels depend on the cellular environment, you would expect that RNA damage would depend only on transcript abundance. But this is not the case. Explain how different RNA types could have different oxidation damage levels?

**Part 3)**

Synthetic modification of 8-oxo-dG in some promoter regions was initially found to activate transcription of reporter genes. Although oxidative stress oxidizes the VEGF promoter, reduced binding of SP1 to G-rich elements increases transcription. What would this result suggest about the role of 8-oxo-dG modification?
