## Prompt

Context: shRNAs are a helpful tool in research, where specific sequences can be targeted in an mRNA strand to prevent the production of that protein. These tools can therefore specifically target protein production, and allow scientists to tease apart protein function.
Question: Part 1. You are trying to determine the function of a certain protein highly expressed in colon cancer by an inducible shRNA with lentivirus. You decide to use MC38 cells for your model but first need a cell line to generate the lentiviral particles.

1.1 What cell line could you utilize to first produce virus?

1.2 Give a shortened version of a potential protocol to test knockdown efficiency in this system.

Part 2. Your laboratory is interested in a particular protein and utilize an undisclosed oncogenic cell line to study this protein, due to the availability to your lab and familiarity with this cell line. The protein of interest is involved in functions such as DNA repair, cell cycle, and stemness. A graduate student decides to knock it down with shRNA.

2.1 The student designs primers to target this protein, located on chromosome 11. Based on this information, what is likely the protein of interest? Give a brief rationale for your answer.

Part 3. In order to test the efficiency of this shRNA system, the student constructs an experiment in 4T1 cells. This experiment includes cell lines produced by the student. Cell line 1 was transfected with shRNA to serve as a control and cell line 2 was transfected with shRNA for Protein1. The cell lines are split into 3 treatment groups, treated with dox for 24, 48, and 72 hours. The cell lysates were processed and then ran on a gel for western blot, where the membranes were treated with antibodies targeting the protein of interest and a housekeeping gene.

3.1 What differences would you expect to see between 24-72 hrs in cells transfected with shRNA for Protein1 on the western blot?

3.2 What differences would you expect to see between the 72hr treatment line between cells expressing shRNA Control and shRNA Protein1 on the western blot?

3.3 What are two downstream markers of the protein of interest that could be worth blotting for in order to assess downstream effects?

3.4 Provide a potential shRNA sequence for Protein1.

Think step by step and solve the problem below. In your answer, you should include all intermediate derivations, formulas, important steps, and justifications for how you arrived at your answer. Be as detailed as possible in your response.

At the end of your response, write your final answer on a new line starting with "FINAL ANSWER".
