## Prompt

A horizontal metal rod has density \(\rho\), length \(L\), circular cross-section radius \(r \ll L\), Young's modulus \(Y \gg \rho g L^3 / r^2\) in a gravitational field of strength \(g\). It is fixed at one end to a wall, which provides a constant vertical force and torque. Define coordinates as follows: \(x\) is the distance along the rod from the fixed end at the wall, and \(y\) is the vertical displacement from the fixed point, \(y > 0\) is upwards. Using suitable approximations, find the function \(y(x)\) which represents the rod's vertical displacement along it, expressed in terms of \(\rho, g, Y, r, x\). Also give the angle, \(\alpha < 0\), between the \(x\)-axis and the rod at \(x=L\), in terms of \(\rho, g, L, Y, r\) as a single approximated fraction.
