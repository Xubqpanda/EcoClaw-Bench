## Prompt

Context: Climate change is increasing the demand for stress resilient crops. Chenopodium pallidicaule, native to the Andes, is an understudied crop with high potential for supporting food security. While Chenopodium pallidicaule may adapt fast to new environments, the plants will encounter new biotic pests and diseases and therefore research into the crop's immune system and disease resistance processes is needed.
Question: You need to establish a robust pathosystem in the novel halophytic crop Chenopodium pallidicaule as quickly as possible to aid the deciphering of its plant immune system. You must propose and justify the selection of a suitable model pathogen for your investigation. Describe how you would screen the virulence of this pathogen quickly, and how you would determine which the most suitable pathovar of this model pathogen is. Describe the logical experimental steps, expected outcomes and justify your proposition of the best pathovar(s) for investigating disease processes in Chenopodium pallidicaule.
